How to Properly Install the Different Motherboard Attachmetnts

Step 1: RESEARCHING

  • Engage your self to learn the diffrent components through researching or reading a guide manual for assemby or installation of the add-on components.
  • Learn the names and functions of each component, the important components to remember are:
    Mother board (MOBO)
    Central Processing Unit (CPU)
    CPU Heatsink or Cooler
    Random Access Memory (RAM)
    Graphic Processing Unit (GPU)
  • Learn the difference of AMD CPU's and Intel CPU's
  • Learn the compatibility requirements of the different components, note that every components have different brands
    and versions that can only work with other compatible components.
  • Learn the proper handling and safety hazards regulation through research or through manuals.
  • Use a anti static wrist-band and make sure to attach it into a conductive material
  • Bring a No. 2 Phillips screwdriver for the installing purposes

Step 2: CPU INSTALLATION

  • First, remove the plastic cover from the top of the socket of the motherboard.
  • Then lift the retention arm, loosen the metal enclosure.
  • Handle the processor by the edge and prevent touching the bottom of the processor.
  • Notice the arrow on one of the edges of the processor and match that with the arrow on the motherboard socket.
  • Install the processor by gently lowering it down into the socket.
  • Move the retention place down to its original position to securely and firmly attach the processor in the socket.
  • After the CPU is installed, apply some thermal paste to the CPU, apply a grain of rice-like quantity on the CPU, spread it and you are good to go.
  • Proceed to the CPU Installation

Step 3: CPU COOLER INSTALLATION

    (For Intel)

  • For Intel Processors, take out the Heatsink and the cooler from the box. Align it along with the holes on the motherboard outside the socket.
  • After aligning the latches of the cooler with the motherboard holes, press the latches against the motherboard diagonally and lock it by rotating the latch screw.
  • Repeat the same method on the opposite side. Hear out for the click sound which will ensure the cooler is perfectly seated and locked with the motherboard.
  • Double-check the cooler to see if the cooler is attached properly or not.
  • Plugin the PWM fan connector to the nearest fan header on the motherboard.

(For AMD)

  • For AMD Processors, take out the heatsink and the cooler from the box. Unscrew and remove the plastic brackets from the motherboard.
  • The metal tray behind the motherboard also comes out once you remove the brackets. Hold the tray with one hand
    and align the cooler with the holes of the motherboard and screw both of them altogether.
  • Once one end is screwed up properly, the rest of the screws can be tightened easily without any issues.
  • Double-check the cooler to see if the cooler is attached properly or not.
  • Connect the PWM fan connector to the nearest fan header on the motherboard and your CPU Cooler is successfully installed.

Step 4:RAM INSTALLATION

  • Consult your motherboard's manual.You want to make sure you're putting the RAM into the correct slots, after all, so you can get the full performance out of them.
  • Open your RAM slots. Each RAM slot will have two small clips at either side. Press these down to open them.
  • Line up your RAM so that the gap on the connector corresponds with the RAM slot.
  • With your RAM lined up, gently press it down into the slot. When the RAM stick is fully depressed,
    the locking tabs at each side should click back into place. Once they have, you're all set.

Step 5:GPU INSTALLATION

  • Power down the PC.
  • Hit the switch on the back of the PC to turn off supply to the PSU.
  • Extract the side panel (usually held on by two screws on the rear).
    If you do not already have a GPU installed, skip to Step 7.
  • Remove the screws holding the GPU in on the rear bracket.
  • Unlock the PCI-e slot clip.
  • Remove the GPU by safely and lighlthy pulling on the card.
  • Moutn the new GPU over PCI-e slot.
  • Push down on the GPU to slide the whole GPU into the slot.
  • Latch down the secure locks into place.
  • Screw the rear bracket down to secure the card to the chassis.
  • Connect any required PSU cables
  • Reattach the side panel.
